## 2. RSI Pattern Research — what we learned

### 2.1 Scope and approach

Investigate the M / C / V topology Dr. A identified visually on RSI(14) charts. Four hypotheses formalized:

- **H1** Detection is reliable
- **H2** Pattern is fractal across timeframes
- **H3** Pattern is predictive of forward DXY returns
- **H4** State transitions are well-modeled by Markov chain (or HMM)

DXY-only for v1. Daily / 4h / 1h / 5m timeframes. BarChart Premier CSV downloads. Welles Wilder RSI with RMA smoothing.

### 2.2 Run 1 — State-occupancy analysis (initial null finding)

H2 partial confirm: state occupancy is fractal-proportional across timeframes (M ~18%, V ~18%, C ~63% on every timeframe). Transition matrices statistically indistinguishable across most pairs. Absolute durations scale with timeframe but proportions don't.

H3 first cut FAILED: state occupancy alone shows tiny effect sizes (|Cohen's d| < 0.2) for forward returns. Conclusion at this stage was "pattern is descriptive, not predictive."

H4 cleanly confirmed: Markov chain (6 params) beats best HMM (20 params) by BIC of 134 units. Direct M↔V transitions are exactly 0 — patterns always traverse through C. State machine matches Hickson-style theory.

### 2.3 Run 2 — Event-conditional transitions (Dr. A's correction)

User pointed out the signal lives in TRANSITION events, not state occupancy. Re-tested: **enormous effect sizes**, all p < 0.001, consistent across timeframes:

- **C→V transition** (RSI first hits trough of V): 1-bar fwd return +0.36% (daily), Cohen's d = +0.91
- **C→M transition** (RSI first hits peak of M): 1-bar fwd return -0.34% (daily), Cohen's d = -0.86

The 1-bar mean-reversion at the extreme bar is robust across daily / 4h / 1h / 5m. Effect concentrates at the 1-bar horizon and decays by 5 bars.

### 2.4 Run 3 — Trough-breach signals (Dr. A's two-tier idea)

Three breach signal candidates tested:

**V-floor breach** (RSI drops below the lower of T1, T2 after V): **strongest single signal in the entire study**.

| Timeframe | 20-bar Cohen's d | Mean fwd return |
|---|---|---|
| Daily | **-1.53** | -2.93% |
| 4h | -1.53 | -1.07% |
| 1h | -1.44 | -0.55% |
| 5m | -1.31 | -0.15% |

This is institutional-grade — Cohen's d > 1.3 across all timeframes for a 20-bar forward return signal.

**M-bottom breach** (RSI drops below structural floor of M after completion): weak short. Daily 20-bar d = -0.18; 1h 20-bar d = -0.39. Useful as confirmation, not primary.

**M-dip breach** (RSI drops below inner dip between peaks during right leg): TURNS OUT to be a LONG signal, not short. Daily 5-bar d = +0.82. Counter-intuitive but consistent across timeframes — DXY rallies after M-dip breach, opposite of naive contrarian intuition.

### 2.5 Run 4 — M lifecycle stats and entry-point comparison

Striking finding: **M duration is nearly identical across timeframes** (~20 bars regardless of TF). Amplitude (~12 RSI units) also TF-invariant. Same for V. This is fractal self-similarity in pattern morphology beyond what state-occupancy showed.

Tested four candidate entry points along the M lifecycle:

| Entry point | Daily 20d Cohen's d | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| P1 (first peak of M) | **+1.44** | Long |
| P2 (second peak) | +0.97 | Long |
| Dip-breach (on right leg) | +0.58 | Long |
| M-bottom breach (after completion) | -0.18 | Weak short |

**Conclusion: M is empirically a continuation-up pattern on DXY**, not a topping reversal. Every early entry into M predicts strong upward continuation. Only the very late M-bottom breach produces a (weak) short signal. The naive "RSI overbought = sell" intuition is empirically incorrect for DXY.

### 2.6 Run 5 — Regime conditioning (does M-short work in bear or high-vol regimes?)

Tested under trend regime (close vs 200-bar SMA) and volatility regime (20-bar rolling stdev). Result: M-shorts don't work in any tested regime. P1 entry produces +2.6% to +3.2% over 20 days in BOTH bull and bear regimes (effect even stronger in bear regime, d=+1.71). Hidden regime where M-shorts work doesn't exist in the unconditional data.

Slight improvement: M-bottom breach gains marginal short edge in bear / high-vol regimes (d=-0.39 → -0.52 on 1h). Doesn't change the strategic conclusion.

### 2.7 Run 6 — Precursors (what predicts an upcoming M/V?)

The 20 bars before M's P1 are characterized by:
- RSI rising steadily (net velocity +0.66/bar vs baseline +0.01, d=+1.0)
- Already in upper-half (16/20 bars above 50, d=+1.1)
- Mean RSI ~61 (d=+1.4 vs baseline)

Symmetric for V (mirror). The precursor IS the left leg — pattern recognition gives no leading signal beyond "RSI is in the direction of an emerging M/V."

### 2.8 Run 7 — M attempt outcomes (when does an RSI cross succeed in forming M?)

When RSI crosses ≥65 from below on DXY, the outcomes are:

| Outcome | Daily | 1h |
|---|---|---|
| M completes | 67% | 66% |
| Single-peak failure (RSI peaks and retraces, no M) | 31% | 31% |
| Extended overbought run | 2% | 3% |

**Critical finding for pre-qualification:** the 5-bar RSI velocity at the cross discriminates outcomes. Slower approaches into 65 → M tends to complete (d=-0.36 daily). Faster spike into 65 → single-peak failure. The discovery that "rate-of-change of the approach matters" is real, but the direction is **slow rise = M complete, fast rise = M fail** — opposite to the naive intuition.

### 2.9 Run 8 — Pattern clustering (NOT alternating)

Disconfirms the "M → C → V → C → M" alternating-cycle intuition. After an M completes, the next pattern is:

| Previous pattern | Next is M | Next is V |
|---|---|---|
| **After M (daily)** | **76%** | 24% |
| After M (1h) | 71% | 29% |
| After V (daily) | 28% | **73%** |
| After V (1h) | 33% | 67% |

**M's beget more M's. V's beget more V's. Patterns CLUSTER by type.** Opposite-type transitions are rare (24-33%). This is trend-clustering at the pattern level. After seeing an M complete, the prior should be "another M is coming" (~3:1 odds), not "now a V is due."

### 2.10 Run 9 — Multi-feature classifier (cleanest tradeable result)

Gradient-boosted tree trained on 10 precursor features. Chronological 70/30 train/test split (no leakage). Target = "M will form in next 30 bars."

| Target | Daily AUC | 1h AUC |
|---|---|---|
| M predictor | 0.684 | 0.683 |
| V predictor | 0.724 | 0.647 |

Out-of-sample forward returns by P(M) decile (daily, 20-day hold):

| Decile | P(M) range | 20d fwd DXY return |
|---|---|---|
| 0 (lowest) | 0.03 | **-1.81%** |
| 5 (median) | 0.24 | +0.25% |
| 9 (highest) | 0.71 | **+0.89%** |

**Monotonic, clean, 2.7% spread top vs bottom decile.** This is the most useful artifact of the project — a calibrated probability that doubles as a directional bias signal:

- **P(M) > 0.5** → bullish bias (long DXY)
- **P(M) < 0.1** → bearish bias (short DXY) — bottom decile delivers -1.81% over 20 days
- 0.1 ≤ P(M) ≤ 0.5 → stand aside

Bottom feature importances: current RSI level (0.37), RSI max in 20-bar window (0.26), RSI min in 20-bar window (0.21), rolling volatility (0.06). The model effectively learns "where is RSI now relative to recent range" — but with proper out-of-sample validation.

### 2.11 Strict-M definition (Dr. A, 2026-05-12)

Dr. A specified a much tighter M topology:
- Origin: RSI rises **from below 30**
- Major peaks: ≥**75.01** at close
- Wiggle troughs: stay ≥**70** (allow multiple peaks in upper zone)
- Completion: RSI drops below 50

Implemented at `src/rsi_pattern/patterns_strict.py`. Detected counts (vs loose definition):

| Timeframe | Loose M | Strict M | Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily | 124 | 4 | 31× fewer |
| 4h | 36 | 3 | 12× fewer |
| 1h | 253 | 31 | 8× fewer |
| 5m | 255 | 32 | 8× fewer |

Strict-M is much rarer. Daily has too few (n=4) to permit statistical analysis. Hourly (n=31) and 5-minute (n=32) are tractable but sample sizes are still tight for hypothesis testing.

Rise-leg characteristics under strict definition:
- Mean rise: ~35 bars from <30 to ≥75.01 (1h, 5m)
- Mean velocity: 1.5-2.0 RSI/bar
- Most strict-Ms are SINGLE peak (no wiggle): 26 of 30 on 1h
- Multi-peak strict-Ms are rare and concentrate on 1h

Rate-of-change feature is now part of `StrictM.rise_velocity`. Earlier analysis (Run 7) on the loose M-attempt definition showed that **fast** RSI rises produce single-peak failures, slow rises produce M-completes. Under the strict definition, every detected strict-M by definition cleared the 75.01 threshold — the question becomes whether rise velocity within strict-Ms differentiates outcomes. Sample is small (n=30-32 on 1h/5m). Further validation requires more data.

## 3. Practical signal hierarchy (synthesis across all runs)

Ranked by Cohen's d and replicability:

**Tier 1 — institutional-grade short signal:**
- V-floor breach. 20-bar Cohen's d = -1.3 to -1.5 across all timeframes. Strongest single result.

**Tier 1 — institutional-grade long signal:**
- M P1 entry on daily 20d hold. d = +1.44, mean fwd return +2.79%.

**Tier 2 — fast 1-bar signals:**
- C→M transition. d ≈ -0.86 to -0.97. Hold 1 bar only.
- C→V transition. d ≈ +0.84 to +1.03. Hold 1 bar only.

**Tier 2 — sustained signals:**
- M P2 / Dip-breach entries (long, d +0.58 to +0.97 on daily 20d)
- M-bottom breach (short, d -0.18 to -0.39 — weak)

**Tier 3 — classifier-based:**
- Out-of-sample classifier P(M) decile filter. 2.7% spread between top and bottom decile on daily 20d. Trade top decile long, bottom decile short, stand aside in the middle.
